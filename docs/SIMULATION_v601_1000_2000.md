## Accuracy Context

Position RMS of 221–275 m with σ = 150 m measurement noise is consistent with theoretical limits:

- Cramér-Rao lower bound at σ = 150 m with 5 s update: ~106 m (single-model, steady state)
- IMM with 6 models adds state estimation overhead from model mixing
- Coordinated turn tracks have inherently higher position uncertainty during manoeuvres
- Commercial cruise contributes lowest error; helicopters and military with high turn rates contribute most

The 1.5–1.8× factor over CRLB is within expected IMM performance for mixed traffic.

## Detection > 100%

Detection rates slightly above 100% indicate a small number of confirmed clutter tracks (5–17 out of 100 total clutter measurements). With 5 clutter/scan and M/N=3/4 confirmation, clutter tracks are expected when random clutter points happen to form consistent trajectories. This is standard radar tracker behaviour — clutter track rate is controlled by the confirmation threshold.

## Notes

- Python and C++ trackers use the same IMM 6-model filter and GNN association
- C++ uses sparse Bertsekas auction algorithm for assignment; Python uses SciPy LAPJV
- OpenMP parallelism contributes ~20% speedup at 2000+ targets
- All measurements are synthetic with realistic kinematics; for live ADS-B data see BENCHMARK_v591_500plus.md
